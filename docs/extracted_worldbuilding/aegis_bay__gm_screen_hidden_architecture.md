## 1. Global History of the Fracture

### The Veil

The Veil was the ontological membrane separating mundane and mythic reality.

It did **not** fail all at once. It broke over centuries through historical crises.

### Broad historical shape

#### Eastern fracture
- Black Death as mass-death rupture
- long-running Eurasian thinning
- Ottoman administrative flexibility later becomes the first large-scale integrated-state model

#### Western fracture / sealing dynamic
- Indigenous peoples maintained sophisticated ward-management traditions
- Cahokia was discussed as a major seal / containment event in some models
- colonial destruction of indigenous ward networks destabilized the hemisphere

#### Industrial acceleration
- pollution, deep mining, rail, and extraction damaged the conditions that made the old veil possible

#### Aegis Bay's local timing
- the Pacific Northwest held out late
- local ward traditions kept the area stable longer than many other regions
- when the collapse came in the 1890s–1910s, it came **late and hard**
- Aegis Bay therefore skipped romanticized emergence and threw magic directly into industrial capitalism

## 2. The Original Sin of the Setting

The **Supernatural / Mythic split** is not a natural taxonomic truth.

It emerged as a labor-and-governance weapon.

### Hidden logic

- multispecies labor solidarity became dangerous to state and capital power
- powered humans were carved out as a more respectable category
- non-human beings were left exposed to regulation and extraction
- bureaucracy hardened this distinction until it felt natural

### Result

The city's modern inequality is not an accident of culture. It is the inherited product of a deliberate political split.

## 3. The 3-Axis Ontological Model

This is the later **creator-only** model that replaced earlier public-facing taxonomies.

### Axis 1 — Integration

**Question:** Who is here?

Tracks demographics, density, and the degree to which mythic / powered populations are socially embedded.

### Axis 2 — Coherence

**Question:** Do the stories agree?

Tracks how strongly shared belief, repeated narrative, media, paperwork, and social consensus crystallize local reality.

Physics stability is not the root; it is a symptom of narrative agreement.

### Axis 3 — Liminality

**Question:** How thin is the boundary to elsewhere?

Tracks how close the boundary feels, how porous places become, and how much "elsewhere" can press against the local world.

## 4. The Veil Mechanic Underneath the Setting

The buried truth of Aegis Bay is that **belief shapes reality through repetition, circulation, and narrative sediment**.

This includes:

- forms filled out thousands of times
- repeated media stories
- social myth-making
- comic-book power archetypes
- public categories becoming materially real through repetition

### Canonical symptom examples from the logs

- Form 274-B physically feels heavier because of accumulated dread and repetition
- repeated news shifts what landlords treat as normal or prudent
- a viral self-story can pull a person's reality closer to the collectively believed version
- institutions change the world by telling stories about it, even when they deny that is what they are doing

## 5. The DHA's Blind Spot

The DHA is not a cabal of secret occult masterminds.

It is a bureaucracy with a measurement problem.

### What it can measure

- registrations
- incidents
- tax revenue
- compliance
- manifested risk
- documented bodies and powers

### What it cannot truly process

- coherence drift
- narrative causality
- culturally generated ontology
- thinness as lived / inherited / ritual knowledge

### Institutional consequence

The DHA counts what it can count and ignores what it cannot turn into policy.

The logs repeatedly framed this as **institutional physics, not cartoon evil**.

## 6. Narrative Contamination

One of the most important hidden concepts was the DHA's refusal to fully acknowledge a phenomenon it has partially observed.

Working phrase from the discussion:

**Narrative Contamination Hypothesis — Unsubstantiated / Do Not Reference**

This expresses several truths at once:

- the DHA has seen the pattern
- the DHA has categorized the pattern
- the DHA has chosen not to build public policy around the pattern
- denial is active and institutional, not naive absence of knowledge

## 7. Why the Prompt Should Not Explicitly Teach the Model the Whole System

This was argued at length in the OpenRouter discussion.

Core conclusion:

If the AI is given the full explanatory model directly, it will:

- start referencing it too explicitly
- flatten mystery into exposition
- make bureaucrats sound like metaphysicians
- lose the grounded, lived-in feeling of the world

So the production approach became:

### What the AI sees
- heavy forms
- weird rental ripple effects
- minor glitches
- institutional denial
- localized folklore, theorists, and fragments

### What the GM knows
- the 3-axis architecture
- the labor-history origin of the split
- the real reason forms and stories matter
- why the DHA is predictably wrong

## 8. Suggested GM Uses

Use this hidden architecture to decide:

- what kinds of consequences make sense after media events
- why one neighborhood destabilizes while another does not
- when a bureaucratic intervention backfires
- why an elder or theorist would read the same incident differently from a DHA analyst
- how repeated mundane actions accumulate metaphysical weight

Use it to design incidents.

Do **not** use it to make every character explain the setting.

---

## 9. Short Hidden-Lore Summary

> The world broke historically, not mythically. Aegis Bay's local veil fell late, so capitalism and industry met emergence head-on. The Supernatural/Mythic divide was manufactured to break solidarity. Reality now runs on integration, coherence, and liminality, but the DHA only meaningfully grasps the first. The city is full of symptoms of story-made-physical, while the institutions trying to govern it are stuck auditing the surface.
