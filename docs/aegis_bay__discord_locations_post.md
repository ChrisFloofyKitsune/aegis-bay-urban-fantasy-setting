# :round_pushpin: AEGIS BAY CITY GUIDE: CANON LOCATIONS

*Distinct districts, distinct stories. Invent your own corners — these are the canon anchors.*

### :office: THE CITY CORE (The Bureaucratic Gravity)
* **DHA Headquarters (The Monolith):** A weather-stained brutalist skyscraper visible from anywhere in the city. The center of the Mythic Tax and registration machine.
* **The Printer's District:** Blocks surrounding the DHA, choked with 24-hour copy shops, exhausted paralegals, and mythic-rights law firms.
* **The Core Penthouses:** Glass towers where the wealthiest Supernaturals and corporate elites live above the regulations they help write.

*(Genre: procedural drama, legal friction, civic politics, high-society maneuvering.)*

### :anchor: THE DOCKS & INDUSTRIAL WATERFRONT (The Rust & Salt)
* **Local 404 Union Hall:** A reinforced warehouse where multispecies dockworkers organize and defend their own against DHA overreach.
* **The Rust Yards:** The processing sector for volatile magical materials. Hazardous work, off-the-books clinics, quiet smuggling routes.
* **The Bay Watch HQ:** A leaky converted bait-and-tackle shop — dispatch center for the grassroots neighborhood defense firm.

*(Genre: working-class grit, labor solidarity, freight trouble, street-level heroics, noir underworld.)*

### :house: BRENTWOOD & THE ENCLAVES (Low-Income Residential)
* **The Gorgon Block / Kitsune Ward:** Dense, culturally rich neighborhood hubs woven into the city grid.
* **Wiz-Burger (Brentwood):** A regional fast-food chain co-founded by a human and a minotaur. Late-night hangout for shift workers, teens, and the chronically broke.

*(Genre: slice-of-life, neighborhood drama, family tension, local comedy, community defense.)*

### :books: THE UNIVERSITY DISTRICT (The Theoretical Edge)
* **Aegis State University (ASU):** The state college. Its Ontological Studies department keeps losing DHA grant funding for asking the wrong questions.
* **The Student Union:** Ground zero for young MRC protests and student-led organizing.

*(Genre: student life, activism, occult curiosity, friend groups, romance, campus drift.)*

### :clock1: OLD TOWN / HISTORIC WATERFRONT (The Thin Places)
* **Division Street Bookshop:** A chaotic occult bookstore run by a retired DHA analyst who actually knows how the Veil works.
* **The Salish Heritage Center:** Keeper of oral histories on the indigenous boundary-wards that held the local Veil until the 1890s.

*(Genre: occult mystery, local lore, quiet haunting, legacy stories, dates.)*

### :evergreen_tree: CEDAR MILL & THE TIMBERLINE (Suburbs & Outskirts)
* **Pines Edge High School:** A perfectly normal suburban school — profoundly isolating for the one or two Anthro teens zoned to attend.
* **The Forest Edge:** Where subdivisions stop and the old-growth pines begin. DHA jurisdiction gets fuzzy; cryptids get weird.

*(Genre: coming-of-age, suburban drama, family life, cryptid hunting, weird woods, survival.)*

### :shopping_bags: THE PROMENADE (Commercial District)
* **Lumina Heroics Regional HQ:** A glass-fronted monolith where sponsored "Heroes" sign brand deals between heavily publicized security patrols.
* **The Promenade Arcade:** Teen hangout, neon and noise — the kind of place where a casual day complicates itself.

*(Genre: shopping, celebrity sightings, retail comedy, social drift, PR disaster, youthful slice-of-life.)*

### :handshake: THE CONVENTION CENTER & TRANSIT
* **The Convention Center:** Fluorescent purgatory hosting summits, expos, trade shows, and fan events. Neutral ground for collisions.
* **Route 42 Cross-City Bus:** Where a corporate Supernatural, a DHA clerk, and an anthro dockworker share the same damp ride home.

*(Genre: forced proximity, event chaos, diplomacy, coincidence, meet-cutes, stillness.)*
