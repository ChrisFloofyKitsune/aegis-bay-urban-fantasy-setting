# Aegis Bay — Current Canon and Prompt Inputs

**Status:** Current / Prompt-Aligned

This document captures the worldbuilding that most clearly fed into the current `world_prompt.md` and the AI handoff.

---

## Core Premise

Aegis Bay is a **high-friction urban fantasy metropolis** on the Pacific Northwest coast, circa 2026.

- The supernatural is **public**.
- Mythics and powered humans are integrated into daily life.
- The city is **rainy, tired, functional, and under strain**.
- The main antagonist is not a dark lord but **bureaucratic management**: the DHA, the Mythic Tax, and privatized heroic response.

The key framing line that kept reappearing is:

> **Aegis Bay is where the seams show.**

## Tone and Aesthetic Rules

### Tone

- grounded
- tired but functional
- friction made visible
- stillness is valid
- neighbors matter
- bureaucracy acts like weather

### Anti-Default Aesthetic Rules

These were repeatedly reinforced during prompt refinement:

- No generic "purple crystal" fantasy leakage
- No glowing-rift slop
- No anime dictatorship visuals for the DHA
- No fast romance escalation

### Approved Visual Register

- contemporary urban fashion
- class-legible modern clothing
- anime-influenced visuals without abandoning mundane realism
- kemono / anthro / elf / giant / gorgon / kitsune bodies normalized in public space
- rust, rain, diesel, wet pavement, moss, cheap offices, fluorescent light

### DHA Visual Register

- cheap suits
- lanyards
- short-sleeved button-downs
- navy windbreakers
- clipboards
- beige-plastic institutional tech
- MRI / TSA / municipal inspection vibes

## The Social Split

The setting's civic divide is **political, not natural**.

- **Supernatural** = usually powered humans; more marketable, more easily branded, easier for institutions to celebrate
- **Mythic** = non-human beings; more likely to be taxed, regulated, monitored, and treated as civic liabilities

This split is foundational to the city's inequality and to the DHA's entire operating logic.

---

## The DHA and Daily Bureaucratic Pressure

The Department of Heroic Affairs is a bureaucratic monolith built around:

- registration
- compliance
- incident management
- taxation
- dispatching heroic contractors
- risk categorization

It does not present itself as evil. It presents itself as **practical, stabilizing, and civic-minded**.

### Signature daily pressures

- the **Civic Accommodation Surcharge / Mythic Tax**
- registration renewals
- permits for manifestation use
- classification reviews
- risk check-ins
- public-facing risk-management language that sounds sterile rather than hateful

### Key prop that emerged from the discussion

**Form 274-B** became the iconic bureaucratic object of the setting.

- It was explicitly discussed as a repeatable tactile prop.
- It later solidified into the Registry Update / Compliance form in prompt-facing material.
- Its unnaturally heavy feel became a performed symptom of the deeper world logic.

## DHA Tracking Structure

The discussion settled on a **two-track** DHA approach:

### 1. Legal / Civic Status

- Unregistered
- Registered / Baseline
- Commercial / Heroic
- Probationary / Conditional

### 2. Risk Designation

- Class 1 Monitored
- Class 2 Hazard
- Class 3 Active Threat

This split mattered because it separated **civic legibility** from **danger potential**.

## Geography as Genre Engine

One of the most important late-stage decisions was that **geography dictates genre**.

Different districts are not just scenery; they are genre selectors.

### Core zones that stabilized

#### The City Core
- DHA HQ
- law firms
- copy shops
- policy, procedural drama, political friction

#### The Docks / Industrial Waterfront
- magical ore / timber legacy
- unions
- rust and labor solidarity
- working-class grit

#### Brentwood / The Enclaves
- low-income walkups, corner stores, laundromats
- mythic neighborhoods as dense community hubs, not isolated ghettos
- where the Mythic Tax is felt in rent-day reality

#### Cedar Mill / Suburbs
- integration that looks successful from outside
- emotionally isolating for the mythic kid who is the only one on the block
- coming-of-age and neighborhood stories

#### University District
- activists, theorists, protests, occult-intellectual pressure

#### Old Town / Historic Waterfront
- thin places
- bookshop theorists
- Salish memory and older local knowledge

#### Convention Center
- forced-neutral ground
- summits, trade shows, bureaucratic collision space

#### Transit Corridors
- bus, ferry, train, shared exhaustion
- connective tissue across class and species lines

#### The Promenade / Commercial District
- added later to support mall / shopping / celebrity / Lumina stories
- clean, power-washed, high-end retail zone
- strong class contrast with Brentwood and the Docks

## Story Start Evolution

The opening structure went through a major pivot.

### Earlier opening concept
- three generated openings based on protagonist type
- more complex, wider cognitive load on the AI

### Final high-value pivot
Every story starts on **the Route 42 Cross-City Bus in the rain**.

This solved several problems at once:

- gave the AI a fixed atmospheric starting room
- placed all classes and species into one believable shared space
- let the world perform itself through small details
- turned genre choice into a physical destination: **"Where are you getting off?"**

### Bus start requirements that stabilized

- scene begins on the bus
- damp coats, diesel, ozone, rain, public transit atmosphere
- protagonist anchored in their loaded details
- companions implied but not overloaded
- one small grounded symptom of the world's deeper logic included

### Stop menu logic

The stop menu eventually expanded into a broad genre engine including:

- Slice-of-Life
- Coming-of-Age / Student Life
- Procedural / Political Drama
- Working-Class Grit
- Corporate Security / PHC
- Active Dispatch / Emergency Response
- Occult Investigation
- Street-Level Conflict
- Academic / Activism
- Noir / Underworld
- Survival / Wild Mythic
- High-Society / Political

The final goal was always the same: give the player **three distinct story-launch vectors** tailored to the protagonist while preserving setting identity.

## Repeated Deep-World Symptoms That Were Kept Prompt-Facing

The logs repeatedly argued that the prompt should not explain the full metaphysical model directly. Instead, it should present **performed symptoms**.

Recurring examples:

- Form 274-B feels heavier than normal paper
- repeated news coverage changes what landlords treat as prudent
- a mythic teen's self-story shifts as it is collectively believed
- a neighborhood micro-zone behaves strangely because the local stories conflict
- the DHA counts what it can count and ignores what it cannot formalize

This "symptoms, not lecture" approach is one of the strongest design through-lines in the whole project.

## High-Confidence Current Canon Summary

If a future draft needs the shortest possible summary of what the logs converged on, it is this:

> Aegis Bay is a rainy Pacific Northwest urban fantasy metropolis where public coexistence is real, equality is not, and bureaucracy does most of the violence. The DHA regulates and classifies; the Mythic Tax shapes daily choices; PHCs commercialize heroism; unions, activists, and neighbors keep people alive. Geography shifts genre, and every story starts on the Route 42 bus in the rain.
