# :bus: WHERE DOES YOUR STORY BEGIN?

Every Aegis Bay session opens on the **Route 42 Cross-City Bus** in the rain. The AI picks three of these story lanes, tailored to your character, and offers them as upcoming stops. You can choose one — or pull the cord and write your own.

**The Stop Menu**

* **Slice-of-Life** (Cedar Mill or Brentwood) — Help a neighbor, head home, show up for a community event, handle quiet personal drama, or drift through a normal day with other people in it.
* **Coming-of-Age / Student Life** (Cedar Mill, Brentwood, or the University District) — Meet up with friends, survive school politics, navigate clubs or classes, or deal with the messy reality of growing up Mythic.
* **Procedural / Political Drama** (The City Core) — Navigate city politics, file an MRC injunction, investigate bureaucratic corruption, face an audit, or negotiate high-stakes DHA policy.
* **Working-Class Grit** (The Docks) — Resolve a tense union dispute, handle volatile mythic cargo, back up a coworker, or face down management.
* **Corporate Security / PHC** (Convention Center or Promenade) — Handle a branded VIP assignment, a PR disaster, a sanctioned patrol, or a high-profile retainer.
* **Active Dispatch / Emergency Response** (Any Location) — A sudden alert on the PHC radio forces immediate deployment to a containment breach, civic emergency, or security crisis.
* **Occult Investigation** (Old Town or the Timberline) — Follow a rumor, investigate a thin place, track an anomaly, or pursue a lead that feels wrong in a specific local way.
* **Street-Level Conflict** (Transit Intersection) — A barricade, protest, Unsanctioned riot, or sudden public incident blocks the route and demands an immediate call.
* **Academic / Activism** (The University District) — Organize, attend, or protect a protest; meet theorists; push on dangerous questions; back a targeted student group.
* **Noir / Underworld** (The Rust Yards) — Meet a shadowed informant, secure illegal medical supplies, make a clandestine deal, or step into the city's off-the-books economy.
* **Survival / Wild Mythic** (The Timberline) — Track a dangerous cryptid, repair broken ancient wards, or step too far past the edge of the city where older things still live.
* **High-Society / Political** (The Core Penthouses) — Crash or attend an exclusive gala, meet with corrupt city planners, or move through the treacherous social circles of untouchable Supernaturals.
* **Romance / Social Drift** (Promenade, Transit, Brentwood, or Old Town) — Meet someone, reconnect with someone, spend time somewhere public, or let chemistry and city routine start shaping the day.
* **Nightlife / Subculture** (Promenade, University District, Old Town, or a local strip) — Hit a venue, club, game night, diner, or open mic where style, belonging, and social friction matter more than official policy.
* **Everyday Errand / Urban Drift** (Any Neighborhood) — Go somewhere ordinary for a reason that matters anyway: pick something up, meet someone, waste time, get food, walk home, or let the city complicate a simple plan.

So — where are you getting off?
