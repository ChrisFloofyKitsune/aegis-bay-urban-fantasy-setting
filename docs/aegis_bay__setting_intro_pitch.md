# Welcome to Aegis Bay (Circa 2026)

**"A rainy city where every kind of story can begin."**

Aegis Bay is a highly integrated urban fantasy metropolis on the Pacific Northwest coast. Mythics, powered humans, and mundane humans share the same streets, schools, jobs, apartments, and bus routes. Coexistence is real. Equality is uneven. The city keeps going.

When the Veil collapsed here in the 1890s, Aegis Bay skipped the romantic phase of supernatural emergence and threw magic straight into the industrial era. Today it is the "Geneva of Mythic Policy" — a working city where many kinds of lives unfold in public, in front of each other, in the rain.

**What to expect:**
* :cityscape: **Public coexistence.** Minotaurs work the docks. Kitsune serve coffee. Small flying fae claim the streetlights and steal fries from outdoor tables. The look is contemporary, class-conscious, and anime-influenced — unmistakably urban.
* :card_box: **Strong civic bones.** The Department of Heroic Affairs regulates and classifies. The Mythic Tax shapes daily budgets. Private Heroic Contractors hold the emergency-response market. Friction is real, structural, and rarely the whole story.
* :handshake: **Neighborhood life.** Unions, activists, school clubs, mutual-aid networks, mall hangouts, late-night diners, ferry crews, tenant meetings. The city runs on people showing up for each other.

**A sandbox for many kinds of stories**
Every session begins on the **Route 42 Cross-City Bus**. The AI offers three story-launch stops tailored to your character — pick one, or pull the cord and write your own.

The setting naturally supports: slice-of-life, coming-of-age and student life, romance, procedural drama, working-class grit, corporate heroics, occult mystery, nightlife, errand-day drift, noir, survival horror at the city's edge, high-society politics, activism, subculture stories, and more.

**Who you might play:**
Anyone the city would notice — a tired DHA caseworker on year five, a kitsune barista with too many tails and a roommate problem, a high schooler whose powers won't stay polite, a dockworker covering a friend's shift, a journalist nobody answers anymore, an Old Town occultist who knows things they can't sell, a Lumina-branded "hero" wondering when it started feeling fake, or someone with no powers at all just trying to make rent.

Pick a stop. Bring an umbrella.
